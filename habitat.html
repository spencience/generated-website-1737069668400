Habitat Quick Facts

  • • Primary Range: Central and South America
  • • Elevation: Sea level to 2,500m
  • • Preferred Canopy: Mid to high levels
  • • Territory Size: 32-86 hectares
  • • Key Countries: Costa Rica, Panama, Colombia

Conservation Status

Status: Least Concern

Population Trend: Decreasing

Natural Habitats of Capuchin Monkeys

Primary Forest Types

Capuchin monkeys inhabit various forest ecosystems across Central and South America. Their adaptability allows them to thrive in:

  • Tropical rainforests
  • Dry deciduous forests
  • Mangrove swamps
  • Gallery forests

Habitat Adaptations

Capuchins have developed remarkable adaptations to their forest environments:

  • Prehensile tails for balanced movement in canopy
  • Excellent spatial memory for food source locations
  • Ability to survive in fragmented forest patches
  • Flexible diet adapting to seasonal availability

Current Threats

  • • Deforestation and habitat fragmentation
  • • Urban development and expansion
  • • Climate change impacts on food availability
  • • Human-wildlife conflict in agricultural areas
