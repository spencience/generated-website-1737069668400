Tool Use & Intelligence in Capuchin Monkeys

Capuchin monkeys demonstrate remarkable cognitive abilities through their sophisticated use of tools. Recent studies have shown they can select appropriate stones based on size and weight for cracking nuts, and even transport tools over distances for future use.

Key Observations

Our research team has documented numerous instances of tool use, including:

Stone Tools

Used for cracking nuts and processing hard foods

Stick Tools

Employed for probing and extracting insects

Research Implications

These findings have significant implications for our understanding of primate cognition and the evolution of tool use in early human ancestors.
