Study Highlights

  • • Duration: 5 years (2019-2024)
  • • Sample Size: 12 groups
  • • Total Subjects: 156 individuals
  • • Location: Costa Rica
  • • Lead Researcher: Dr. Maria Santos

Key Findings

  • • 87% maintain long-term friendships
  • • 92% show reciprocal grooming
  • • 73% form cross-generational bonds

Social Bonds Study Results

Our groundbreaking 5-year study reveals fascinating insights into capuchin monkey social relationships and group dynamics. The research demonstrates unprecedented levels of social complexity and emotional intelligence among these remarkable primates.

Major Findings

Friendship Patterns

Strong evidence of lasting friendships spanning multiple years, with preferred partners maintaining consistent bonds through various life stages.

Social Learning

Young capuchins show remarkable ability to learn social customs and tool use through observation of bonded group members.
